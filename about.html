Omar's Parents

When I was born, my dad looked at my mom and said, “I’m going to get some milk.” He never came back. Just like that, he vanished from my life — no memories, no contact, just an empty space where a father should’ve been.

That means I only have one parent. But it’s not what people imagine when they hear that. My mom is abusive. She insults me constantly, tells me I’m not a man, says I’m useless. There’s no kindness in her words — only sharp edges.

She won’t even let me use the internet at home, not even to study. If I want to check something for school, I have to go outside in the heat and connect to public Wi-Fi. I stand there, sweating and tired, just to log into Outlook and see what I’m supposed to learn.

This page isn’t for sympathy. It’s just the truth — part of my world, and why I made this site in the first place.
